💡 The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysia, Tenera oil palms are vital for food security (cooking oil, food additives). However, land is scarce. To optimize food production without further deforestation, plantation managers must know exactly when a tree's growth rate peaks and when its yield velocity slows down to plan replanting cycles efficiently.

Realistic Reference Data: Inspired by empirical growth profiles from the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) Tropical Agri-Bio Informatics datasets.

📐 The Calculus Model

Yield Function $f(x)$
$$f(x) = -0.012x^3 + 0.36x^2 + 0.5x$$

Where $f(x)$ is the Fresh Fruit Bunch (FFB) yield in tonnes per hectare per year, and $x$ is the tree age in years (from year 2 to 25).

The Derivative $f'(x)$ (Rate of Change)
$$f'(x) = -0.036x^2 + 0.72x + 0.5$$

$f'(x)$ measures the instantaneous production acceleration velocity (change in yield per added year).

💡 Food Security Insight: When $f'(x) = 0$, the crop has hit its maximum absolute biological yield. When $f'(x)$ turns negative, the tree is consuming more soil nutrients relative to what it outputs, directly indicating a threat to long-term resource efficiency.
